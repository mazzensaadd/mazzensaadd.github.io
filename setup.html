My Setup

Laptop:

Currently, I am using an Ideapad 330 Lenovo laptop with an i7 8th gen quad-core CPU and 4 GB onboard DDR4 + 4 GB DIMM. It’s actually more computing power than what I normally need so I am pretty satisfied with it. The one thing that I hate about my laptop is the Nvidia Geforce MX150 GPU which I faced lots of issues getting it to work properly with my operating system of choice.

Operating System:

My criteria for choosing an operating system:

  • Must be Free as in Freedom.
  • Code must be Open-Source.
  • Only contain the kernel & core utilities when installed.
  • Allows me to control my machine with my own mindset and workflow. I don’t want someone else to tell me what to do or how to do it.
  • Easy to use (doesn’t mean it’s easy to learn, don’t be a peasant).
  • Respects my fucking privacy.

This list goes on forever so I will end it here but you got an idea of what I am searching for. My Operating system of choice is GNU/Linux. I am running Arch Linux. Arch is User-Centric not User-Friendly. It’s not trying to appeal to as many users as possible. Instead, It gives users the ability to build everything from the ground up. One should just RTFM.
